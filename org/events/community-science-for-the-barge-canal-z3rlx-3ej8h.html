Community Science for the Barge Canal

  • Sunday, November 20, 2022
  • 3:00 PM 5:00 PM
  • Pine Street Barge Canal 501 Pine Street Burlington, VT, 05401 United States (map)
  • Google Calendar ICS

All are welcome (from newbie naturalists, to amateurs to professionals) to join us in discovering who in the trophic web lives on this fragile urban wild and to record it for a baseline inventory. This includes: mammals, reptiles, amphibians, flora, fungi, invertebrates, birds...

This parcel of land on Unceded Abenaki Territory is a Brownfield Site abutting a Superfund Site and has the potential to become a long term remediation - restoration -rematriation education center.

So join us if you can one or any Sunday (2nd and 4th of each month) from May-November.

Bring binoculars, hand lenses, field guides, your iPhone for pictures and iNaturalist entries, and your enthusiasm.

Since the site is a Brownfield Site, there will be a simple waiver to fill out. The only precautions you will need to take are to: not wear the shoes you wear in the field in your home for 2 few days (just leave on the porch to air out), do a thorough tick check afterwards, and of course tend to your hydration, sun protection, and snack needs.

Park across the street at Dealer.com or behind the Maltex building. Enter 501 Pine Street Burlington VT on your GPS. For anyone with mobility challenges who wants to attend, please let us know your needs and we will do all we can to support your participation.

Lets come together to learn, witness our wild kindred living in this fragile habitat, and record what we can for robust deliverables to share at the end of autumn 2022 as a bridge between our human and nonhuman urban wild neighborhoods in need of deep care.

Children are welcome!
If you have a naturalist specialty, feel free to reach out to Jess to email located on: (www.mycoevolve.net) and you can lead a tier of the group that day to focus on your specialty.

This event is hosted by MycoEvolve in collaboration with Friends of the Barge Canal.
